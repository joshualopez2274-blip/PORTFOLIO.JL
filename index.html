Bowl Liner

Complete Reverse Engineering Workflow

Step 1: Field Measurement

Challenge: Slightly worn 800kg liner with irregular surfaces due to abrasion.

Approach: Employed calipers, measuring tape, and digital inclinometer to capture 30 internal ribs.

Step 2: Detail Capture

Engineering Insight: Internal ribs are critical for the fitting and locking mechanism; analyzed and measured ribs that showed no signs of wear.

Step 3: CAD Reconstruction

Solution: Reconstructed geometry in Autodesk Inventor and generated 2D drawings with tolerance specifications.

Outcome: Zero dimensional rejections on first production pour.

Jawplate Crushers

Reverse Engineering & Field Measurements

Step 1: Wear Geometry Analysis

Challenge: Worn jawplates with significant material loss, making original dimensions difficult to determine.

Step 2: Dimensional Reconstruction

Engineering Insight: Utilized remaining non-worn surfaces and symmetry to reconstruct the jawplate's profile in CAD.

Step 3: Material & Design Optimization

Solution: Created a 3D CAD model with optimized profiles for improved crushing efficiency and specified Manganese steel alloy.

Outcome: Produced manufacturing-ready drawings.
